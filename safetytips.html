Personal Safety Guidelines

Walking Safety

Always walk in well-lit areas, especially after dark
Stay alert and avoid distractions like headphones in isolated areas
Walk confidently and be aware of your surroundings
Use campus security escort services when available
Carry a whistle or personal alarm for emergencies
Share your location with trusted friends when going out
Avoid shortcuts through isolated or poorly lit areas

Digital Safety

Keep emergency contacts saved and easily accessible
Install safety apps with location sharing features
Keep your phone charged and carry a portable charger
Be cautious about sharing personal information online
Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication
Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions

Accommodation Security

Access Control

Always lock doors and windows when leaving your room
Never share access codes or duplicate keys
Report lost keys or access cards immediately
Don't let strangers into the building
Check security cameras and lighting in common areas
Use door chains or peepholes before opening doors

Protecting Valuables

Keep valuable items in a secure lockbox or safe
Don't leave electronics or cash visible
Take photos of serial numbers for insurance
Consider getting renters insurance
Mark your belongings with your name or student ID
Report suspicious activities to security immediately

Fire Safety Protocols

Prevention Measures

Test smoke detectors monthly and replace batteries
Keep fire extinguisher accessible and learn how to use it
Don't overload electrical outlets or use damaged cords
Keep cooking areas clean and never leave food unattended
Never use candles or open flames in accommodation
Don't disable smoke detectors even if they're sensitive

Evacuation Procedures

Know all evacuation routes and emergency exits
Practice evacuation routes regularly
Stay low to avoid smoke and use stairs, never elevators
Meet at designated assembly points outside
Feel doors before opening - if hot, find another exit
Never go back inside for belongings

Emergency Procedures

Medical Emergencies

Steps to take during health-related emergencies

1

Assess the Situation

Check if the person is conscious and breathing. Don't move them unless they're in immediate danger.

2

Call for Help

Dial 112 for emergency services or campus health services at +234 800 555 0456.

3

Provide First Aid

Only if trained. Otherwise, wait for professional help and keep the person comfortable.

4

Stay with the Person

Monitor their condition and provide reassurance until help arrives.

Security Incidents

How to respond to theft, break-ins, or suspicious activities

1

Ensure Your Safety

If you witness a crime in progress, don't intervene. Get to a safe location immediately.

2

Contact Authorities

Call campus security (+234 800 555 0123) or police (112) depending on severity.

3

Document Details

Note time, location, description of suspects, and any other relevant information.

4

Report to UniLodge

Inform our support team so we can take additional security measures if needed.

Natural Disasters

Procedures for earthquakes, severe weather, and flooding

1

Stay Informed

Monitor weather alerts and emergency broadcasts on radio or mobile apps.

2

Take Shelter

Move to the safest area of your building - away from windows and on lower floors if possible.

3

Emergency Kit

Keep water, non-perishable food, flashlight, and first aid supplies readily available.

4

Wait for All-Clear

Don't leave shelter until authorities confirm it's safe to do so.
